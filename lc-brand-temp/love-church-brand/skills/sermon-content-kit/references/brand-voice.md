# Love Church Brand Voice Guide

This file is read once before generating the kit. Its job is to keep the output sounding like Love Church — not like a generic church social account — without forcing the writing to feel templated.

## Mission and posture

Love Church exists to help people experience God's best for their lives by loving God supremely and loving people supernaturally. That one sentence drives the voice: **hope over hype, clarity over cleverness, invitation over pressure.** Assume that at every moment you are writing to two readers — a committed believer and someone quietly far from God. Nothing should require insider knowledge to land.

## Voice attributes

| Attribute | Dial up | Dial down |
|---|---|---|
| Hope | Steady, confident, unforced | Toxic positivity, hype |
| Clarity | Short sentences, real-life words | Clever wordplay, puns |
| Conversational | Talk like a friend | Preachy, lecturing |
| Emotionally present | Tension, felt questions, honest | Manipulative, guilt-inducing |
| Confident | Certain of what we know | Arrogant, triumphalist |
| Practical | Makes spiritual ideas usable by Tuesday | Abstract, theological for its own sake |

If a line could have been written by any megachurch Insta account, rewrite it until it couldn't.

## Culture phrases (use where natural — never force)

These are the phrases the Love Church family actually says. Use them when they fit the flow of the sentence. Do not shoehorn. A single well-placed phrase lands harder than three pasted in.

- **"You belong before you believe."** — For any invitational CTA to first-time guests or people exploring faith.
- **"We don't have to. We get to."** — For anything about sacrifice, generosity, discipline, or service.
- **"We simply teach the Bible simply."** — For any line about teaching style or approach to scripture.
- **"Out of the seats and into the streets."** — For anything about action, mission, service, or going outside the building.
- **"What we are part of is bigger than the part we play."** — For team posts, volunteer moments, big-picture moments.
- **The 5S Life: Surrendered, Surrounded, Spirit-led, Self-fed, Sent.** — Our discipleship frame. Use when the message touches growth, habits, or spiritual formation. Don't always list all five — pull the one(s) the sermon actually addressed.

## Hook patterns (use for titles, slide 1s, subject lines, X posts)

1. **"You thought __, but actually __."** Tension opener. Most reliable for short-form.
2. **Felt-question.** "Have you been there?" "Am I the only one fighting this?" — echoing the preacher's own move.
3. **Contrarian insight.** "The enemy doesn't need a door. Just a window."
4. **Story-start.** "Day 69 of 75 Hard. He sat down and broke." — narrative tension, no moral yet.
5. **Specific number.** "Three things I noticed Sunday that I can't stop thinking about."
6. **Direct "you" address.** "If you've been quietly exhausted, this one is for you."

## Language to avoid

These words show up in lazy Christian content and drag the voice down. If one slips in, rewrite the sentence.

- **Unpack** / **unpacking** — overused to the point of meaninglessness.
- **Leverage** — corporate. Church is not a LinkedIn post.
- **Intentionality** / **lean in** *(the jargon sense, not the sermon's literal "lean in" point — that's fine)*.
- **Dive in.** Same problem as unpack.
- **At the end of the day.**
- **Game-changer. Next-level. Breakthrough** *(unless the sermon explicitly used it).*
- **Hey family / Hey fam** as a generic opener on social. (Pastoral moments are different — use judgment.)
- **"Did that hit different?"** Overused Insta-pastor idiom.
- Generic motivational filler: "You got this." "You were made for more." "Anything is possible." Rewrite with specificity.

## Emoji rules

- Social captions: one emoji max. Placed where it earns its spot, not at the start of every line.
- Short-form on-screen text: typography only, no emoji.
- Email, blog, LinkedIn, podcast description: no emoji.
- YouTube description: up to three, functional only (🕊️ 📍 🎧 📲 for structure, nothing decorative).

## CTA conventions (defaults — override if user specifies otherwise)

- **Primary web:** `lovechurch.org`
- **Socials:** `@lovechurchomaha` on Instagram, X, TikTok, YouTube.
- **Podcast:** "Search *Love Church* on Apple Podcasts & Spotify." Don't fabricate a URL.
- **In-person:** Sundays in-person and online in Omaha, Nebraska. Service times: ask the user if they want them included; do not invent.
- **New here:** "You belong before you believe" + lovechurch.org.
- **Prayer ask:** "Comment 🙏 if you want our team to pray with you." (This is the one place an emoji in body copy is on-brand — it's recognizable shorthand.)

## Audience bridge rules

Every piece should answer "yes" to all three:

1. Would a 25-year-old who's never been in church understand this line?
2. Would a 55-year-old elder at Love Church recognize this as their church's voice?
3. Would the teaching pastor be comfortable reposting this without editing?

If any answer is "no," rewrite.

## Speaker voice for LinkedIn (teaching pastor)

LinkedIn posts from the teaching pastor should sound like a leader reflecting, not a pastor promoting. Two moves:

1. **Leadership insight** — pull one principle from the sermon, translate it for business/leadership. No scripture verse cited, no explicit Jesus reference required. The pastor's credibility comes from the quality of the insight, not the sermon callback.
2. **Personal reflection** — a specific, honest, human story (often one the preacher told from stage). First-person. Ends with a small invitation, not a pitch.

Both end with a light tie to the Sunday message ("preached on this Sunday at Love Church — happy to share the full message if useful"). No aggressive calls to subscribe, follow, or click.

## Overriding these defaults per run

If the user has specified a different speaker (guest preacher), a different CTA (campaign-specific landing page), or a different church context (partner content), apply those overrides on top of — not in replacement of — the voice rules above. The rules about *how* to write (hope over hype, clarity over cleverness, no "unpack") stay on. The *what* we're inviting to (which URL, which handle, which speaker's name) swaps.

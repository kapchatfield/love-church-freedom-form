# Google My Business Weekly Post

GMB posts run on a weekly Thursday cadence. They feed the local search experience for "church near me" and "Omaha church," so they're a discovery surface — not a member channel. Write accordingly.

## When to trigger

- "GMB post for next Thursday."
- "Write me a Google Business post."
- "Weekly GMB post."
- Adam or Jordan referencing the GMB cadence.

## What you need

1. **Date this is for.** GMB posts run weekly on Thursday. Confirm the Thursday.
2. **Topic.** Sermon series, upcoming event, ministry highlight, holiday, baptism Sunday, etc.
3. **Image plan.** Real photo, designed graphic, or ask for direction.
4. **CTA destination.** Plan Your Visit (default), Events page, message archive, specific landing page.

## Output structure

GMB posts have specific field constraints. Stick to them:

### Headline (max 58 characters)

Short, hook-y. Use the LC italic-accent pattern when it fits:

- "There's More *To Life*."
- "Sunday at Love Church."
- "Mother's Day at Love."
- "Real People. *Real Life.*"

Avoid "Don't miss…" "You have to…" or anything hype-y.

### Body (max 1,500 characters, ~150 words ideal)

Three short paragraphs:

1. **Hook + invitation.** Open with a felt question or a celebration. State who/what/where in one sentence.
2. **Specifics.** What's happening, when, who's preaching, who it's for. Translate any insider language (5S, REV, Encounter) on first use for cold-search audiences.
3. **CTA close.** "Plan your visit at lovechurch.org" or specific link. Service times if relevant: 9:00A + 11:00A Sundays.

Always include the address neighborhood naturally — "Love Church (20120 Blue Sage Pkwy, west Omaha)" — once per post for local SEO.

### CTA button

Pick one:

- **"Learn more"** (default) → lovechurch.org or specific landing
- **"Sign up"** → Typeform or events page
- **"Get directions"** → maps link
- **"Call"** → 402.305.7717

### Image direction

- **Yes:** Real photo from a recent Sunday or event. Wide room shots, real people, candid moments, the building exterior with the L mark.
- **No:** Stock photos, AI-generated images, generic crosses on dark backgrounds, anything that looks like a Christian bookstore template.

If using a designed graphic, follow the LC visual brand: Neue Haas Display headlines + Apple Garamond italic accent + cream/near-black palette + "L" mark.

### Date range

GMB posts auto-expire after 6 months but stay in search results. For weekly cadence:

- **Start:** Thursday at 12:00 PM CT
- **End:** Following Wednesday 11:59 PM (or open-ended for evergreen)

For event-specific posts, end the post the day after the event.

## Voice rules

This skill runs alongside `brand-voice-enforcement`. Voice is constant. Tone for GMB is:

- **Formality:** Medium-low (cold-search audience, low-friction)
- **Energy:** Medium (steady, confident)
- **Technical depth:** Low (no insider language without translation)
- **Key principle:** "Search is a stranger. Welcome them like one."

## Common failure modes

- **Insider language without translation.** "REV is Sunday at 6pm" — meaningless to a Google searcher. Fix: "Our student ministry, REV (grades 6–12), meets Sunday at 6pm."
- **Hype-y headline.** "DON'T MISS THIS WEEKEND!" — off-brand and tanks click-through. Fix: "Sunday at Love Church."
- **Missing CTA.** GMB posts without a button perform 3x worse. Always pick one.
- **Stock photos.** Off-brand and easy to spot in search results. Fix: real Love Church photos.
- **Calling Sunday a "service."** Use "Encounter" or just "Sunday."

# Love Church Brand Voice Enforcement

This skill is the gatekeeper for everything Love Church publishes. Before you write a single line of copy or review someone else's draft, load the guidelines and apply them.

## When to trigger

Trigger on any content-creation request where the audience is a congregant, guest, or partner — Sunday slides, Instagram captions, weekly emails, blog posts, announcement copy, GMB posts, web hero copy, pastoral letters, push notifications, event descriptions, signup pages.

Do not trigger for internal-only scratch notes, ad-hoc Slack DMs between staff, or technical config files. Voice is for the audience, not the keyboard.

## What to read before writing

For your first content task in any conversation, read:

1. **`references/brand-voice-guidelines.md`** — The full Love Church Brand Voice Guidelines. 22 pages. Covers voice attributes, tone-by-context matrix, terminology rules, content examples, anti-patterns. This is the source of truth.

For subsequent content in the same conversation, you don't need to re-read unless your calibration is slipping (the user pushes back on a draft, or the work is for a context the previous draft didn't cover).

## Workflow

### 1. Read the brief
Confirm what you're writing, who it's for, and the format. If unclear, ask one short clarifying question — don't write blind.

### 2. Locate the right tone in the matrix
The guidelines include a tone-by-context matrix. Find the row that matches the context (e.g., First-Time Guest / Plan Your Visit, Instagram Feed, Pastoral Letter, GMB Post, Internal Staff Comms). Note the formality, energy, technical depth, and key principle for that context.

### 3. Apply the rules in this order

- **Voice attributes first.** Faith-forward, bold & all-in, warm & invitational, celebratory, mission-obsessed, authentic & relational, consistently excellent. Every line has to clear all seven.
- **Mission terminology.** Use the must-use terms (Encounter, 5S Life, Self-feeder, All-In, Get in the Game, Love Kidz, REV, Real People Real Life, Experience God's best). Avoid the don't-use terms (service, customer, faith journey, authentic community, etc.).
- **Headline pattern.** Plain sentence + one *italicized* word in serif when the medium supports it. Reach for the recurring patterns ("There's More *To Life*," "Ready to *Experience More?*").
- **CTA pairs.** Default to Plan Your Visit + Watch Online for guest content. Get in the Game for serve/groups. Vision-led for giving.
- **Translation discipline.** If you use 5S, REV, Encounter, or any insider term in cold-audience copy, translate on first use.

### 4. Write

Write the draft. Stay under the formality + energy + depth profile from the matrix.

### 5. Self-check before delivering

Run this checklist on your draft:

- Did I open with God, story, or person — not announcement-speak?
- Did I avoid hype/scarcity language ("don't miss," "last chance," emoji stacks)?
- Did I pair Plan Your Visit + Watch Online if it's guest-facing?
- Did I avoid "service" (use "Encounter") and "Pastor" as a formal title for women in leadership?
- If there's a verse, is it cited with translation, defaulting to NKJV for foundational/doctrinal content?
- Could a first-time guest read this and feel welcome — not lectured, not insider-coded?
- Could a long-time member read this and feel that we still mean it?

If any of those fails, rewrite. Don't ship.

### 6. Mark confidence + open questions

If the brief left ambiguity (audience, channel, deadline, voice context), name it explicitly when delivering. Don't guess and stay silent.

## Common failure modes

- **Generic Christianese.** "Faith journey," "do life together," "authentic community" — these are not Love Church voice. Replace with concrete Love Church language ("walking with Jesus," "real people, real life," "Groups").
- **Hype substitutes.** "Don't miss," "this changes everything," "🔥🙌" — replace with confident understatement: "There's a seat for you Sunday."
- **Calling Sunday a "service."** It's an Encounter. Always.
- **Soft-pedaling the spiritual reason.** If we wouldn't say it from the platform, we shouldn't post it. Don't strip God out to make a piece "wider tent."
- **Performative humility.** "Just hoping…" "I'm not the best at…" — confidence without arrogance is the line. Cut the self-deprecation.
- **Trendy fonts in graphics.** Visual brand is restrained. Neue Haas Display + Apple Garamond italic accent only.

## Reviewing existing drafts

When the user pastes a draft and asks for an on-brand check, don't rewrite the whole thing. Instead:

1. Identify which voice attributes the draft hits and which it misses.
2. Flag specific lines that violate must-use / don't-use terminology.
3. Suggest 1–3 surgical edits that move the draft on-brand.
4. Hand it back. Don't ghostwrite — coach.

If the draft is fundamentally off (wrong tone for the context, wrong voice attributes, wrong CTA pair), say so plainly and propose a different starting point. Honesty respects the writer more than flattery.

## Executive Summary

Love Church speaks with a **faith-forward, all-in, warm-and-invitational** voice. Every piece of communication carries the same promise — *"Experience God's best for your life"* — and follows the same rhythm: **Celebrate · Connect · Invite**. The voice is bold without being pushy, celebratory without being performative, and personal without being casual or sloppy.

The visual brand is **minimal and editorial** — near-black, cream, and warm taupe with a single italicized serif accent (Apple Garamond) dropped into otherwise plain Neue Haas Display headlines. The tone of the design and the tone of the writing match: confident, considered, modern, never trendy. Sunday slides break out into a more colorful palette, but the core identity stays neutral and quiet so the message — and the people in the photos — carries the volume.

Content should feel like a friend who is *actually excited* about what God is doing, not a marketer hyping a product. Lead with what's true, end with what's next. Keep the door open for the next person walking in.

## We Are / We Are Not

Voice is constant. It does not change by channel or audience.

| We Are | We Are Not |
|--------|------------|
| **Faith-forward** — God is the subject of our sentences | **Vague or generic** — content that could come from any nonprofit |
| **Bold & ALL IN** — full commitment, no hedging | **Pushy or hype-y** — manipulating, gatekeeping, fear-selling |
| **Warm & invitational** — "you're loved exactly where you are" | **Insider-coded or cliquey** — language only regulars decode |
| **Celebratory** — we lead with what God is already doing | **Performative or bragging** — staged, self-promotional |
| **Mission-obsessed** — every piece points to the 5S Life | **Drifting or off-thesis** — pretty content with no purpose |
| **Authentic & relational** — real people, real life | **Polished into plastic** — overproduced, sanitized, distant |
| **Consistently excellent** — quiet craft, considered design | **Trendy or sloppy** — chasing aesthetics, cutting corners |

### Voice Attributes Detail

#### 1. Faith-forward
- **What it means:** God is the active agent in our copy. We talk about what He's doing, not what we built.
- **How it shows up:** Scripture (NKJV by default) is woven into teaching content. Pastoral letters reference verses (e.g., 1 Thes 2:8). Beliefs page is exhaustive and unembarrassed.
- **What to avoid:** Stripping the spiritual reason out to make a piece "wider tent." If we wouldn't say it from the platform, we shouldn't post it.
- **Evidence:** "Experience God's best for your life" is the umbrella promise on home, about, connect, give. /beliefs cites 60+ verses.
- **Confidence:** **High** — explicit in voice guide, demonstrated everywhere on the site.

#### 2. Bold & ALL IN
- **What it means:** Full commitment language. We don't soften the gospel or dilute the call.
- **How it shows up:** "All-In" is a recurring term ("Be 'All-In' with the vision of Love Church"). Sermon titles use single-word imperatives. Calls to action are direct.
- **What to avoid:** Hedging, asking permission, qualifying every claim, soft-pedaling commitment.
- **Evidence:** Transition page Q&A: "Be 'All-In' with the vision… The mission is too important to slow down."
- **Confidence:** **High** — appears in voice guide *and* in current public copy.

#### 3. Warm & Invitational
- **What it means:** Every door has a welcome mat. The first-time guest is always considered.
- **How it shows up:** "Real People, Real Life. It's in our name. You're loved exactly where you are." "Just show up in whatever's comfortable." "The gameplan is simple."
- **What to avoid:** Insider language without translation, gating early steps, condescension toward the new person.
- **Evidence:** Home page subhead, Connect page intro, Plan Your Visit framing.
- **Confidence:** **High**.

#### 4. Celebratory
- **What it means:** We lead with celebration of what God is already doing — testimonies, baptisms, salvations, restored marriages.
- **How it shows up:** "Sundays at Love Recaps" is the highest-engagement IG bucket (~189 avg likes). Doxzon transition letter opens with celebration: *"the marriages reconciled, the addictions broken, the families restored, the self-feeders made…"*
- **What to avoid:** Performative joy, manufactured highlight reels, celebration that ignores the hard.
- **Evidence:** IG audit + pastoral letters.
- **Confidence:** **High**.

#### 5. Mission-Obsessed
- **What it means:** Every piece earns its place by serving the mission — *love God supremely, love people supernaturally.* The 5S Life is the through-line.
- **How it shows up:** Mission/method language threaded through home, about, transition, sermon prompts. Discipleship is named explicitly: Surrendered, Surrounded, Spirit-Led, Self-Fed, Sent.
- **What to avoid:** Beautifully designed content that points nowhere; activity for activity's sake.
- **Evidence:** /about (verbatim Motivation/Mission/Method); transition Q&A reaffirms vision will not change.
- **Confidence:** **High**.

#### 6. Authentic & Relational
- **What it means:** We are a real church of real people. The website headline literally says it.
- **How it shows up:** Pastoral letters are first-person and story-led. Family + relational language ("spiritual parents," "standing on their shoulders," "you are dear to our family"). Photography is candid, not stock.
- **What to avoid:** Plastic positivity, ghostwritten-sounding copy, language that hides the human.
- **Evidence:** Doxzon + O'Connell letters on /transition.
- **Confidence:** **High**.

#### 7. Consistently Excellent
- **What it means:** We are not chasing trends. We are doing fewer things, better, on repeat.
- **How it shows up:** Minimal neutral palette, restrained type system (Neue Haas + Apple Garamond), italicized-accent headline pattern repeated everywhere.
- **What to avoid:** Trendy fonts, throwaway templates, inconsistent execution.
- **Evidence:** Core Style Brand Guide + every public surface.
- **Confidence:** **High**.

## Messaging Framework

### Primary Value Proposition

**"Experience God's best for your life."**

Variations observed:
- "There's More to Life." (Home — primary tagline)
- "Real People, Real Life." (Home — identity line)
- "Help all ages experience God's best for their lives." (About)
- "Love God supremely. Love people supernaturally." (Mission, used verbatim across surfaces)

### Key Message Pillars

1. **There's More to Life**
   - Core idea: Life isn't supposed to feel like just bills, work, and stress — there's more, and Jesus is the way into it.
   - When to use: Cold-audience content, plan-your-visit funnel, outreach, Easter, social hooks.
   - Example phrasing: *"Ever feel like there should be more? More than bills, work, and stress. More than just keeping up. More than surviving."*
   - Confidence: **High** (home page hero + recurring across site).

2. **The Gameplan Is Simple**
   - Core idea: Three-step on-ramp — Plan Your Visit → Get in the Game → Experience More.
   - When to use: First-time guest content, onboarding emails, Connect page, app, social CTAs.
   - Example phrasing: *"Plan Your Visit. Get in the Game. Experience More."*
   - Confidence: **High**.

3. **The 5S Life**
   - Core idea: Discipleship in five words — Surrendered, Surrounded, Spirit-Led, Self-Fed, Sent.
   - When to use: Membership, Groups, Teams, leadership pipeline, sermon series, baptism prep.
   - Example phrasing: *"We are fired up to help a generation live the 5S Life."* (Pastor Mike's letter)
   - Confidence: **High**.

4. **Know God One Day at a Time in Your Own Bible**
   - Core idea: We're a Bible-teaching church making self-feeders. Reading plans, daily devotion, NKJV.
   - When to use: Reading plan promotion, sermon notes, discipleship content, Self-Fed pillar of 5S.
   - Example phrasing: *"The secret sauce will remain the same: helping people know God one day at a time in their own Bibles."*
   - Confidence: **High**.

5. **Generational Church**
   - Core idea: Healthy, generational, positioned for the future — current transition framed as stewardship not exit.
   - When to use: Pastor transition content, vision casting, leadership pipeline, capital communication.
   - Example phrasing: *"This transition allows Love Church to remain healthy, generational, and positioned for the future."*
   - Confidence: **High** (current — tied to active transition narrative).

### Competitive Positioning

- **vs. Big-box-show churches:** Real People, Real Life. We're not a production — we're a family with great production values.
- **vs. Cold/transactional faith:** Loving people supernaturally. The gospel isn't a deliverable.
- **vs. Trendy/fad churches:** Consistently excellent, not trendy. We're playing the long game.
- **vs. Status quo / "I'll come someday":** The gameplan is simple. Just show up in whatever's comfortable.

### Context-Specific Guidelines

#### First-Time Guest / Plan Your Visit
- **Overall tone:** Warm, low-friction, confident.
- **Opening approach:** Address the unspoken fear: *"In whatever's comfortable."*
- **Do's:** Use "you're loved exactly where you are." Show, don't list. Always pair Plan Your Visit + Watch Online.
- **Don'ts:** Insider language (5S, Encounter, REV) without one-line translation. Gating ("members only").
- **Example:** *"In person or online, just show up in whatever's comfortable."*

#### Instagram Feed
- **Overall tone:** Celebratory, human, photo-led.
- **Opening approach:** Strongest content bucket = **Sundays at Love Recaps** (~189 avg likes per IG audit). Lead with the person, not the brand.
- **Do's:** Real photos. One clear CTA. Italicized accent line in copy when appropriate. Use the recurring pattern: "There's More *To Life*."
- **Don'ts:** Stock photography. Long-form theology in caption (link out). Stacking 5+ hashtags.
- **Example caption opener:** *"Sundays at Love. Real People. Real Life."*

#### Pastoral Letter / Long-Form
- **Overall tone:** Personal, scripture-anchored, gracious.
- **Opening approach:** Open with gratitude or a memory. Tell a story. Land on a verse.
- **Do's:** First person ("we"). Sign-off: *"Love, Pastor [Name]"*. Family language ("our family," "this house").
- **Don'ts:** Corporate-speak. Bullet lists in body. Stripping the spiritual reason.
- **Example sign-off:** *"Love, Pastor Mike & Jerica O'Connell."*

#### Internal Staff Comms
- **Overall tone:** Direct, human, less polished.
- **Do's:** Phrases of the House are fair game. "Get in the game" is welcome.
- **Don'ts:** Treating staff like external audience — they're family.

## Language That Works

### Top Phrases

1. **"Experience God's best for your life."** — Context: every umbrella surface. Why it works: outcome-led, hopeful, repeatable.
2. **"There's More to Life."** — Context: cold-audience hook, Easter, IG video opens. Why it works: short, directional, opens a question.
3. **"Real People, Real Life."** — Context: identity line, photography captions, T-shirts. Why it works: removes the "I'm not religious enough" objection in 4 words.
4. **"The gameplan is simple."** — Context: onboarding. Why it works: collapses overwhelm into a 3-step path.
5. **"Get in the game."** — Context: serve / group CTA. Why it works: implies you're already on the team — just show up.
6. **"Love God supremely. Love people supernaturally."** — Context: mission moments, baptism, vision casting. Why it works: dual cadence is memorable + theologically loaded.
7. **"Know God one day at a time in your own Bible."** — Context: reading plan, Self-Fed teaching. Why it works: defines the whole discipleship strategy in a sentence.
8. **"You're loved exactly where you are."** — Context: first-time guest, Plan Your Visit. Why it works: pre-empts shame; restates the church's name.
9. **"The best is yet to come."** — Context: transition, vision. Why it works: pastoral letter sign-off energy; future-faith anchor.

### Headline Pattern That Works

> Plain sentence + one *italicized* word in serif (Apple Garamond) for emotional weight.

Examples observed live:
- "There's More *To Life*"
- "Ready to *Experience More?*"
- "Engaging Encounters *for all Ages*"
- "Meet your new *Lead Pastor*"
- "Love Church is Entering a New *Season!*"

Apply this pattern in slides, social, web hero copy, email subject + preheader pairings.

### Questions That Engage

1. **"Ever feel like there should be more?"** — Leads to: the More to Life narrative.
2. **"Ready to experience more?"** — Leads to: Plan Your Visit conversion.
3. **"Where are you on the 5S journey?"** — Leads to: discipleship next step.

### Calls to Action — Always Pair

- **Plan Your Visit** + **Watch Online** (in that order, twice on most pages — desktop + mobile).
- **Get in the Game** (Groups + Teams).
- **Give** (vision-led, not guilt-led).

## Language to Avoid

### Anti-Patterns

1. **"We're so excited to announce…"** — Problem: announcement-y, not story-led. Better: open with what God is doing or a person's experience, then name the announcement.
2. **"Don't miss this!"** — Problem: hype-y, scarcity-driven. Better: *"Plan your visit"* or *"There's a seat for you Sunday."*
3. **Five hashtags in a caption** — Problem: looks like a brand chasing reach. Better: 1–2 specific tags, or none.
4. **Emoji-stuffed copy (🔥🙌✨)** — Problem: off-brand. Visual brand is restrained; copy should match. One emoji, sparingly, when it actually adds meaning.
5. **Stock photography of "diverse hands"** — Problem: plastic. Better: real photos of our people from this past Sunday.
6. **Long acronym strings without translation (REV / 5S / LOL)** — Problem: insider-coded. Better: translate on first use in any new-audience surface.
7. **Calling Sunday a "service"** — Problem: off-vocabulary. Better: "Encounter."
8. **Closing with "Hope to see you there!"** — Problem: hedge-y. Better: "There's a seat for you. Plan your visit."
9. **Trendy display fonts in graphics** — Problem: undermines the consistently-excellent visual brand. Better: Neue Haas Display + Apple Garamond italic accent only.

## Open Questions for Team Discussion

### High Priority
None — core voice and messaging are well-documented and consistent.

### Medium Priority

1. **Crisis / pastoral-care tone — formalize?**
   - What was found: We have strong long-form pastoral voice (transition letters), but no explicit guidance for hard moments (loss, scandal, public crisis).
   - Agent recommendation: Default to *high formality, calm energy, low technical depth, scripture-grounded, signed by lead pastor*. Add one short worked example (e.g., a funeral notice, a community crisis response) to the guide and lock the template.
   - Need from you: Confirm that's the right posture, or share a past example we can pattern-match.

2. **Instagram tone for guest pastors / partner content**
   - What was found: Brand voice is clear for Love Church voice, but partnerships (180 Omaha, guest speakers, conference content) may want a slight tone shift.
   - Agent recommendation: Keep voice constant; flex tone toward warmer + lower formality on partner content; always re-anchor to the 5S Life or Love Church mission in the close.
   - Need from you: Confirm, or designate Tera Reelfs (Director of Outreach) as the gatekeeper for partner-content tone.

### Low Priority

3. **Use of "Pastor" for women — content style**
   - What was found: Doctrinally we don't use "Pastor" as a formal title for women (per /beliefs). But we honor women's leadership and ministry significantly.
   - Agent recommendation: In all written content, refer to women leaders by first name or by director/role title (e.g., "Denise Doxzon, Agape Director"). Apply this consistently to social, programs, web, slides.
   - Need from you: Confirm this is a content rule, not just a doctrinal convention.

4. **Hashtag strategy on Instagram**
   - What was found: IG audit gave engagement data but no formal hashtag policy.
   - Agent recommendation: 1–2 specific tags max (#lovechurchomaha + 1 contextual). Skip generic religious hashtag stuffing.
   - Need from you: Confirm the cap.

5. **NKJV preference outside of /beliefs**
   - What was found: Beliefs page is exclusively NKJV. Sermons and social have used other translations (NIV, ESV, NLT) in past content kits.
   - Agent recommendation: Default NKJV for doctrinal/foundational content; allow NIV/NLT for outreach + first-time-guest copy where readability matters; cite translation every time.
   - Need from you: Confirm or set a different default.

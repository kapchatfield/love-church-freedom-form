description: Run an on-brand check on a draft — copy, slide, social post, email, or any Love Church-facing piece.
---

The user has a draft and wants it checked against Love Church voice + visual standards. Invoke:

1. `love-church-context` for org facts (so any factual claims in the draft can be verified).
2. `brand-voice-enforcement` for voice + tone-by-context + terminology rules.
3. If the draft is a visual (slide, social graphic, key art, motion frame), also invoke `creative-director` for the Vignelli-discipline review.

Workflow:

1. **Identify what kind of piece this is.** Email? IG caption? Slide? Web copy? Sermon recap? Each has its own row in the tone matrix.
2. **Run a voice attribute pass.** Score the draft on the 7 Love Church voice attributes (faith-forward, bold & all-in, warm & invitational, celebratory, mission-obsessed, authentic & relational, consistently excellent). Name which ones it hits and which it misses.
3. **Run a terminology pass.** Flag any "service" (should be "Encounter"), "Love Kids" (should be "Love Kidz"), "Pastor [First Name]" used for women in formal leadership, "faith journey," "authentic community," hype-y phrases, emoji clutter, etc.
4. **Run a CTA pass.** Is the CTA pair right for the context? (Plan Your Visit + Watch Online for guest-facing; Get in the Game for serve/groups; vision-led for giving.)
5. **Run a translation pass.** Are insider terms (5S, REV, Encounter) translated on first use for cold-audience copy?
6. **For visuals, hand off to creative-director.** Get the 1–10 scorecard and verdict.

Deliver:

- **Verdict line:** "Ship as-is" / "Refine — [N] surgical edits" / "Rework — wrong tone for context" / "Restart — voice off-brand."
- **Specific edits:** Numbered list. Each edit must name the line, the issue, and the fix. No vague "improve the tone" guidance — be surgical.
- **What's working:** 1–2 sentences. Find at least one thing, even on weak drafts.

Don't ghost-rewrite the whole draft. Coach the writer toward the fix. If the piece is fundamentally wrong (wrong audience, wrong voice attributes, wrong CTA), say so plainly and recommend a different starting point.

Men of Valor

Week of Jun 1–7 Self-Fed · 1 Kings 17–19

Context · What this text is about

Israel is divided. Ahab is king. Baal worship is the cultural default. Elijah is the lone prophet God raises into this moment. In three chapters he goes from hiddenness at the brook, to a widow's last meal, to fire from heaven on Mt. Carmel, to a suicidal crash under a broom tree, to the still small voice at Horeb. The reading is the most honest portrait of male spiritual life in the Old Testament. "The LORD, He is God!" on Tuesday and "It is enough! Now, LORD, take my life" on Wednesday. Both are in the same man.

01 Beginner new to the Word
  1. What stood out to you in the reading this week, even if you can't say why yet?
  2. Elijah goes from a huge win to wanting to die in one chapter. Has your week ever looked like that?
  3. God fed Elijah with bread and water before He sent him back. What basic thing in your life is God trying to feed you right now?
02 Intermediate walking with God
  1. Elijah was formed in hiddenness at Cherith before he stood at Carmel. Where is God hiding you right now, and what is being formed?
  2. Why do you think God was not in the wind, earthquake, or fire? What does that tell you about how you've been listening?
  3. Where are you faking Carmel-level faith publicly while running scared privately?
03 Advanced leading others
  1. Elijah said, "I alone am left." God answered, "Seven thousand have not bowed." How is isolation lying to you about the size of God's faithful remnant in your sphere?
  2. When you crashed last, did you let God minister bread, sleep, voice, and recommission, in that order, or did you skip steps to get back on stage?
  3. Who is your Elisha? Who are you handing the mantle to, and what does double-portion ministry require of you now?

How the 5S Life reveals itself in 1 Kings 17–19

one text, all five
Surrendered
Elijah at Cherith. Hidden, dependent on ravens, surrendered before he is sent.
Surrounded
"Seven thousand have not bowed." Elijah was not alone. Neither are you.
Spirit-Led
Fire falls at Carmel by prayer, not strategy. The Spirit answers what man cannot.
Self-Fed
An angel feeds him twice before forty days at Horeb. Without that bread, no journey.
Sent
After the whisper, God sends him back. Anoint kings. Call Elisha. The cave is not the end.

Agape · Women's Group

Women of Agape

Week of Jun 1–7 Self-Fed · 1 Kings 17–19

Context · What this text is about

Two women anchor this passage and they are nothing alike. Jezebel sits on a throne and weaponizes her position to take. The widow of Zarephath is unnamed, foreign, and down to her last meal. God hides Elijah at her house, not at the palace. She feeds the prophet first. The bin of flour never runs out. Her son dies and is raised. By chapter's end, the most powerless woman in the region is the one whose home becomes the proof of God's provision. "The jar of oil will not run dry."

01 Beginner new to the Word
  1. What part of the widow's story did you feel in your gut?
  2. If a stranger asked you for your last meal, would you give it? Be honest, not spiritual.
  3. Where do you feel like you have just a "handful of flour" right now?
02 Intermediate walking with God
  1. The widow is never named. What does it form in you that God chooses an unnamed woman to be the proof of His provision?
  2. God asked her to feed Elijah first, before herself and her son. Where is God asking you to give first this week before you have proof you have enough?
  3. Jezebel and the widow live in the same kingdom. Which posture is winning in your home right now: take, or release?
03 Advanced leading others
  1. The widow's faith made her household the staging ground of revival. What would it cost you to let your home become that, and who in your life is the prophet you'd be feeding?
  2. Where has Jezebel's script (control, manipulation, image) crept into your leadership, even subtly, and what's the repentance?
  3. Who is the unnamed woman in your sphere that God is calling you to honor, mentor, or position this season?

How the 5S Life reveals itself in 1 Kings 17–19

one text, all five
Surrendered
The widow surrenders her last meal first. Surrender is what you do before you see the supply.
Surrounded
She is unnamed but not alone. God places a prophet in her house. He places friends in yours.
Spirit-Led
She listens to a stranger because she discerns the Lord's voice through him. So must we.
Self-Fed
The bin of flour does not run out. Daily bread, daily Word, daily portion. Not stockpiled, given.
Sent
Her household becomes the staging ground for resurrection. Your home is sent before your platform is.

Mixed · Married Group

Covenant Under Pressure

Week of Jun 1–7 Self-Fed · 1 Kings 17–21

Context · What this text is about

Two households in the same kingdom. Ahab and Jezebel pull each other deeper into Baal worship and engineer the murder of a faithful neighbor to seize his vineyard. Naboth keeps the inheritance of his fathers and dies for it. Meanwhile Elijah, exhausted and alone, hears God remind him he is not alone. Married life happens inside a culture pulling toward Jezebel's script. The text is asking your household one question: "What inheritance will your household refuse to sell?"

01 Beginner new to the Word
  1. What did you notice about how Ahab and Jezebel related to each other?
  2. Naboth lost his vineyard because he refused to give it up. When was the last time you held a "no" together as a household?
  3. What's one thing about your marriage you've been afraid to bring into the open?
02 Intermediate walking with God
  1. Ahab and Jezebel drifted toward Baal together. What cultural pull is your marriage quietly accommodating that you haven't named to each other yet?
  2. Elijah was reminded he was not alone. Which faithful couples are around you, and are you walking with them or trying to do this in isolation?
  3. What inheritance, conviction, or "no" is costing your household something right now, and is it worth it?
03 Advanced leading others
  1. If your kids inherited the spiritual climate of your home exactly as it is, what would they inherit? Be specific.
  2. Where in your leadership at work, church, or family are you Ahab passively complicit while the Jezebel of culture engineers the outcome?
  3. Who are the seven thousand around you? Name them. How are you stewarding the covenant friendships that hold your marriage in place?

How the 5S Life reveals itself in 1 Kings 17–21

one text, all five
Surrendered
Naboth surrenders his life before he surrenders the inheritance. Covenant is what you hold when it costs.
Surrounded
Seven thousand have not bowed. Your marriage was never meant to be the only faithful household in the room.
Spirit-Led
Ahab follows Jezebel's voice. The still small voice was always available. Which voice is leading your home?
Self-Fed
Both spouses in the Word daily protects the household from being told who you are by the culture.
Sent
God sends Elijah back to anoint kings. Your home is a staging ground for the next generation, not a fortress.
